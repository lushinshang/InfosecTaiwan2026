### <span id="ai-vs-ai">10:30 - 11:10 \| AI vs AI: Autonomous Cybersecurity, Trust, and the EU AI Act</span>
* **地點**：福祿廳 (主議程)
* **講者**：Joseph Carson (Segura Chief Evangelist & Advisory CISO)
* **講者背景**：
  Joseph Carson 是一位屢獲殊榮的資訊安全專業人員，在企業安全領域擁有超過 30 年的經驗。身為 Segura 的首席安全佈道師和諮詢資安長 (CISO)，他專注於身分安全、網路韌性，並協助組織建立與商業風險相契合的安全策略。Joseph 擁有 CISSP 和 OSCP 認證，曾為政府、關鍵基礎設施和全球企業提供強化現代網路威脅防護的諮詢。他的專業領域涵蓋身分與存取管理、零信任、威脅情資和安全維運。他是《Cybersecurity for Dummies》的作者，該書在全球被超過 50,000 名專業人士閱讀，他也是《華爾街日報》和《Dark Reading》等媒體的定期撰稿人。Joseph 同時主持了《Security by Default》播客，分享關於新興威脅、資安領導力以及建立安全第一文化的洞察。
  
  ---
  *Original English Version:*
  Joseph Carson is an award-winning cybersecurity professional with over 30 years of experience in enterprise security. As Chief Security Evangelist and Advisory CISO at Segura, he focuses on identity security, cyber resilience, and helping organizations build security strategies aligned to business risk.
  A CISSP and OSCP-certified expert, Joseph has advised governments, critical infrastructure, and global enterprises on strengthening their defenses against modern cyber threats. His expertise spans identity and access management, zero trust, threat intelligence, and security operations.
  He is the author of Cybersecurity for Dummies, read by over 50,000 professionals worldwide, and a regular contributor to publications such as The Wall Street Journal and Dark Reading. Joseph is also the host of the Security by Default podcast, where he shares insights on emerging threats, cybersecurity leadership, and building a security-first culture.
* **議程內容**：
  網路安全正在進入一個新階段，攻擊者和防禦者都越來越多地由具備自主決策能力的 AI 系統驅動。同時，各國政府與監管機構也正競相為 AI 的開發、部署、治理及安全性建立護欄。
  本場次將探討 AI 對抗 AI 網路安全快速崛起的現實，其中攻擊與防禦系統在機器速度下運作，而歐盟《AI 法案》等法規也開始形塑組織如何管理風險、問責、透明度與信任。
  攻擊者正利用 AI 自動化偵察、產生極具說服力的釣魚郵件、利用漏洞並動態調整攻擊方式。防禦者則在部署 AI 驅動的偵測、自動化回應、行為分析與以身分為中心的控制，以比人類團隊更快的速度應對威脅。
  隨著 AI 的導入加速，組織必須在維持人類監督與問責的同時，平衡創新、安全與合規。未來的網路安全將不只是人類在防衛系統，而是愈來愈多 AI 與 AI 之間的攻防。
  
  ---
  *Original English Version:*
  Cybersecurity is entering a new phase, one where both attackers and defenders are increasingly powered by autonomous, decision-making AI systems. At the same time, governments and regulators are racing to establish guardrails for how AI is developed, deployed, governed, and secured.

  This session explores the rapidly emerging reality of AI vs AI cybersecurity, where offensive and defensive systems increasingly operate at machine speed and where regulations such as the European Union’s EU AI Act are beginning to shape how organizations manage risk, accountability, transparency, and trust.

  Attackers are already leveraging AI to automate reconnaissance, generate highly convincing phishing campaigns, exploit vulnerabilities, and adapt attacks dynamically. Defenders, meanwhile, are deploying AI-driven detection, automated response, behavioral analytics, and identity-centric controls to counter threats faster than human teams alone can respond.

  As AI adoption accelerates, organizations must balance innovation, security, and regulatory compliance while maintaining human oversight and accountability.

  The future of cybersecurity will not simply be humans defending systems, it will increasingly be AI defending against AI.
